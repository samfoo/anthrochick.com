books & crafts

business facing mobile application

Brief

Books & Crafts creates personalised stationary. They're a small business who are transitioning to something bigger. As the business continues to grow they face greater difficulties tracking orders and managing clients.

Books & Crafts wanted to improve their order managment, but the owner is always on the road. They asked me to rapidly prototype a mobile application to solve their problem.

My Role

User Experience Consulant

Duration

1 week

Tools

  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Illustrated customer journey
  • Low fidelity, pen-and-paper mockups
  • User testing

Iterating and interviewing quickly

Because one week is such a short time, I relied on an extremely light weight approach. I created an interactive prototype that could be quickly changed and tested with the user.

Understanding the dilemma

I spent time interviewing the client to understand how she currently takes an order. From this, I broke the workflow down into three stages and illustrated them.

  1. Client is a small business owner
  2. She travels light, relying on her memory when taking orders in the field.
  3. She often forgets the details of orders and has to reconfirm them.

Modeling the business user flow

In the interview with the business owner, it was revealed that she rarely carries anything other than her phone. The solution was obvious: create a mobile app to track orders. This could also offer her a way to show examples of existing work and upsell.

“ By the end of the night, I can't remember the order. It's embarassing because I have to approach the client again... ” - Juanny Pereira

Ultra light weight, interactive, prototype

After getting an understanding of her workflow and identifying how a mobile app might help, I created a light weight interactive prototype. (Interact with the full prototype)

Design Features

  • Large buttons
  • Opportunities to go back
  • Includes all aspects of the order
  • Allows access to gallery to showcase previous work
  • Allows for monitoring progress on each product

Resulting workflow

Now the client can take orders from anywhere

She uses her phone to track more orders than she could before, and she's able to fulfill them more quickly.
