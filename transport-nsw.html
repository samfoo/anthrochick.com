transport nsw

prototyping a mobile app to increase ridership

Brief

Transport for NSW is responsible for improving the customer experience, planning, program administration, and regulation for all transport services within the Sydney metropolitan area.

Transport for NSW is collaborating with the city of Sydney to encourage and support the usage of public transport during off-peak hours. They approached us to find ways of improving the rider experience and to turn non-riders into riders. The deadline for the program is in 2030, so there's lots of time to experiment.

Researching

Because of the open-ended nature of the brief, we decided to have our preliminary research rely almost entirely on interviews.

After the interviews, we performed a contextual inquiry with real transit passengers. We (politely) approached passengers at one of the busiest stations in the Sydney CBD and spoke to them in the context of a real transit situation.

“ ... weekends, particularly after midnight, you should be careful of not getting into a fight in a bus or train ... ” - Bus and train rider

Contextual inquiries to observe real interactions

Brand
86% say that transport is too unreliable. 70% of infrequent riders considered public transport unclean and dangerous. 50% found public transport confusing.
Tech
No offical app. Over 30 availible unofficial apps (of varying quality) show that there is a strong demand for transport apps.

Personas to highlight likely users

We created personas to highlight those people who are likely to use transport, what their barriers were and how we could help them.

“ Busses have a tendency to be late. Very late... ” - Bus rider

Prototyping an app for contextual navigation

Based on the research above, we decided to rapidly prototype an app that would solve the navigation concerns of users. An official app would also provide the opportunity for Transport for NSW to reach out to riders and alert them of track issues or to report dangerous situations.
